# Smart Water Purification System

## Problem Statement

Access to clean drinking water is still a challenge in many places, and water quality is often difficult to judge visually. I wanted to explore whether a low-cost system could monitor water quality while also demonstrating a basic purification process.

## Project Objective

The goal of this project was to build a smart water purification and monitoring system capable of checking water quality using TDS sensing, displaying readings, and demonstrating a filtration workflow.

## Proposed Solution

I built an Arduino-based prototype integrating a TDS sensor, LCD display, water pump, and filtration setup.

The system monitors water quality, displays readings in real time, and demonstrates water movement through a filtration process.

## How It Works

1. Water enters the purification setup.
2. The TDS sensor measures water quality.
3. TDS readings are displayed on an LCD screen.
4. Water passes through filtration material (cotton/filter medium).
5. A water pump controls water movement through the system.

## Challenges Faced

This project took more trial and error than I expected.

Getting stable TDS readings consistently was difficult, and small setup changes sometimes affected the results. Managing water flow and ensuring the pump worked reliably during repeated tests also took multiple iterations.

I also realized that making a prototype work is very different from making something reliable enough for practical, long-term use.
